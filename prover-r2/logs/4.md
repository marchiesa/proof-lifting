## Why This Is Hard

Dijkstra's correctness proof is one of the most complex algorithm proofs to formalize because:
- The greedy choice requires reasoning about **all possible paths** and showing they're all at least as long as the current minimum
- Positive edge weights are essential (the argument fails for zero-weight edges)
- The interaction between the visited set, distance array, and the min-extraction creates a complex invariant that's hard to maintain through Dafny's verification
- The `IndexOf` function for edge weight lookup creates technical complications
